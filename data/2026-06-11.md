Motivation: 高雷诺数下多维耦合Burgers方程的解随时间演化会产生尖锐激波前沿，传统网格数值方法（如有限差分、有限元）在求解陡峭梯度时存在稳定性差和强网格依赖性问题，因此需要一种更鲁棒的求解方法。

Method: 提出了一种改进的物理信息神经网络框架，包含两个核心策略：(1)层级自适应加权策略，在训练过程中动态调整物理残差、初始条件和边界条件的惩罚权重；(2)双阶段优化策略，实现更稳定的收敛。

Result: 数值实验表明，所提框架在相对L2误差范数上优于标准PINN（无论是否使用L-BFGS优化），并且能够成功捕捉解中随时间演化的尖锐激波前沿。

Conclusion: 层级自适应加权与双阶段优化的组合有效提升了PINN对高雷诺数耦合Burgers方程激波问题的求解精度和稳定性。

Abstract: This paper presents an improved physics-informed neural network for simulating the spatio-temporal solution profile of the multidimensional coupled Burgers' equations with high Reynolds numbers. As time evolves, the sharp shock fronts emerge in the solution, creating significant computational challenges for the conventional mesh-based numerical methods. In particular, numerical methods such as finite differences and finite elements suffer from poor stability and strong mesh dependency when resolving the steep solution gradients. To address these challenges, the proposed framework employs a layerwise self-adaptive weighting strategy that dynamically adjusts the penalty weights for the physics residual, initial conditions, and boundary conditions throughout training. Moreover, the framework uses a dual-phase optimization strategy to achieve more stable convergence.
  To check the effectiveness and accuracy of the proposed framework, a set of numerical experiments is conducted to compare it with the standard Physics-Informed Neural Network (PINN) with and without Limited-memory Broyden-Fletcher-Goldfarb-Shanno (L-BFGS) optimization. Numerical results exhibit that the proposed framework achieves higher accuracy in terms of relative $L_2-$ error norm than the standard PINN and is able to capture the development of sharp shock fronts as time evolves in the solution.
