### [1] [On the duality between consensus problems and Markov processes, with application to delay systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.22554)
*Fatihcan M. Atay*

Main category: math.PR

TL;DR: 该论文将多智能体系统共识问题与马尔可夫过程对偶化，建立统一框架处理时延问题，研究信息传播和处理时延两种类型，推导共识条件和计算共识值。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 将多智能体系统共识问题与马尔可夫过程建立对偶关系，通过两个领域的知识交换，构建统一框架来处理时延问题，解决现有方法在处理时延方面的局限性。

Method: 基于多智能体系统与马尔可夫过程的对偶关系，建立统一数学框架，分别研究信息传播时延和信息处理时延两种类型，通过理论分析推导共识条件和共识值计算方法。

Result: 为两种时延类型分别推导出达到共识的条件，并提供了计算共识值的方法，在统一框架下解决了时延对多智能体系统共识的影响问题。

Conclusion: 通过建立多智能体系统与马尔可夫过程的对偶关系，成功构建了处理时延的统一框架，为时延条件下的共识问题提供了系统性的解决方案。

Abstract: We consider consensus of multi-agent systems as a dual problem to Markov processes. Based on an exchange of relevant notions and results between the two fields, we present a uniform framework which admits the introduction and treatment of time delays in a common setting. We study both information propagation and information processing delays, and for each case derive conditions for reaching consensus and calculate the consensus value.

### [2] [A Single-loop Stochastic Riemannian ADMM for Nonsmooth Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.22750)
*Jiachen Jin,Kangkang Deng,Hongxia Wang*

Main category: math.OC

TL;DR: 提出了MARS-ADMM算法，这是首个具有可证明近最优复杂度保证的随机黎曼交替方向乘子法，用于解决黎曼流形上的非光滑随机优化问题。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有随机黎曼算子分裂方法存在复杂度较高（O(ε^{-3.5})）且通常需要计算昂贵的双循环框架的问题，需要开发更高效的算法来缩小随机黎曼算子分裂算法与非线性约束非光滑随机优化方法之间的理论复杂度差距。

Method: 提出MARS-ADMM算法，结合动量方差缩减梯度估计器（仅应用于目标函数的平滑部分），精心设计的惩罚参数和对偶步长更新策略，采用单循环框架，每次迭代仅需常数次随机梯度评估。

Result: 在温和假设下，MARS-ADMM实现了迭代复杂度为Ō(ε^{-3})，优于先前随机黎曼算子分裂方法的最佳边界O(ε^{-3.5})，与确定性非光滑黎曼优化的最佳已知边界相匹配。

Conclusion: 该算法填补了随机黎曼算子分裂算法与非线性约束非光滑随机优化方法之间的理论复杂度差距，证明仅使用常数大小随机样本即可达到确定性水平的精度。

Abstract: We study a class of nonsmooth stochastic optimization problems on Riemannian manifolds. In this work, we propose MARS-ADMM, the first stochastic Riemannian alternating direction method of multipliers with provable near-optimal complexity guarantees. Our algorithm incorporates a momentum-based variance-reduced gradient estimator applied exclusively to the smooth component of the objective, together with carefully designed penalty parameter and dual stepsize updates. Unlike existing approaches that rely on computationally expensive double-loop frameworks, MARS-ADMM operates in a single-loop fashion and requires only a constant number of stochastic gradient evaluations per iteration. Under mild assumptions, we establish that MARS-ADMM achieves an iteration complexity of \(\tilde{\mathcal{O}}(\varepsilon^{-3})\), which improves upon the previously best-known bound of \(\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^{-3.5})\) for stochastic Riemannian operator-splitting methods. As a result, our analysis closes the theoretical complexity gap between stochastic Riemannian operator-splitting algorithms and stochastic methods for nonsmooth optimization with nonlinear constraints. Notably, the obtained complexity also matches the best-known bounds in deterministic nonsmooth Riemannian optimization, demonstrating that deterministic-level accuracy can be achieved using only constant-size stochastic samples.
